# Northbridge Retail Group - Employee Leave and Customer Refund Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy governs two related but distinct areas of Northbridge Retail Group operations: paid leave for employees and the refund and return process for customers. It applies to all full-time and part-time employees classified as regular staff, and to all purchases made through Northbridge storefronts, the Northbridge website, and authorized marketplace listings. Seasonal and contract staff are covered by the leave sections only to the extent described in Section 3. This document supersedes all prior leave and refund policies issued before January 2026.

## 2. Definitions

"Regular employee" means any worker scheduled for at least 20 hours per week for a period of 90 consecutive days or more. "Benefit year" means the twelve-month period beginning on an employee's hire-date anniversary. "Return window" means the number of calendar days from the delivery date within which a customer may initiate a return. "Store credit" means a non-cash balance issued to a customer's Northbridge account, redeemable only for future purchases and not transferable to another account.

## 3. Paid Time Off Accrual

Regular employees accrue paid time off, referred to here as PTO, at a rate of 1.25 days per month of active employment, reaching the standard annual total of 15 days after a full benefit year. PTO accrual begins on the first day of employment, but newly hired employees may not use accrued PTO until they complete a 90-day probationary period. Unused PTO carries over into the next benefit year up to a maximum of 5 days; any balance above 5 days at the end of the benefit year is forfeited unless local law requires payout. Employees who separate from the company are paid out for unused, accrued PTO up to the 15-day annual cap, less any days already taken in excess of the accrued balance.

## 4. Sick Leave

Sick leave accrues separately from PTO at a fixed rate of 8 days per benefit year, credited in full on each hire-date anniversary rather than accruing monthly. Sick leave may be used for the employee's own illness, a medical appointment, or to care for an immediate family member who is ill. Unlike PTO, sick leave does not carry over between benefit years and is not paid out upon separation from the company. Employees absent for more than 3 consecutive sick days must provide a physician's note before returning to work.

## 5. Parental Leave

Northbridge provides 12 weeks of paid parental leave to regular employees following the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child, provided the employee has completed at least 6 months of continuous service before the leave begins. Parental leave is paid at 100 percent of the employee's regular base pay and does not draw down the employee's PTO or sick leave balances. Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the qualifying event and may be split into two separate blocks of at least 2 weeks each, subject to manager approval of the schedule.

## 6. Bereavement and Jury Duty Leave

Employees receive up to 5 paid bereavement days per qualifying event for the death of an immediate family member, defined as a spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, or sibling. For other relatives, 2 paid bereavement days are available per event. Jury duty leave is paid in full for the entire duration of service with no cap on the number of days, provided the employee submits the court summons to their manager and returns a certificate of attendance at the end of service.

## 7. Requesting Leave

PTO and sick leave requests of 2 days or fewer may be submitted through the employee portal with at least 24 hours notice where practical. Requests of 3 or more consecutive days require manager approval at least 2 weeks in advance, except for sick leave, which may be requested with no advance notice for sudden illness. Parental leave requests should be submitted at least 30 days before the expected start date whenever the timing is foreseeable, to allow staffing coverage to be arranged.

## 8. Product Return Window

Customers may return most items within 45 days of the delivery date for a full refund to the original payment method. Items must be in unused, resalable condition with original packaging and any included accessories; items showing signs of use may be offered a partial refund or store credit at the discretion of the receiving store. Gift purchases may be returned within the same 45-day window measured from the delivery date, with the refund issued as store credit rather than to the original purchaser's payment method.

## 9. Refund Methods and Timelines

Approved refunds to a credit or debit card are processed within 5 to 7 business days of Northbridge receiving the returned item at a fulfillment center. Refunds to store credit post immediately upon return inspection and do not require the 5-to-7-day processing window. Store credit issued under this policy expires 12 months after the date it is issued and cannot be extended or reissued after expiration. Orders placed using a combination of a gift card and another payment method are refunded to the gift card first, up to its original balance, with any remainder returned to the other payment method.

## 10. Non-Returnable Items and Exceptions

Opened electronics carry a 15 percent restocking fee unless the item is defective, in which case Section 11 applies instead. Perishable goods, gift cards, and items marked "Final Sale" at checkout are not eligible for return under any circumstance. Custom or personalized items are non-returnable unless they arrive damaged or materially different from what was ordered. Face masks, undergarments, and swimwear may not be returned once the hygiene seal has been removed, for health and safety reasons.

## 11. Damaged or Defective Items

Items that arrive damaged or that fail within the first 90 days due to a manufacturing defect are eligible for a full refund or free replacement, at the customer's choice, with no restocking fee and no deduction for signs of use caused by the defect itself. Customers should report damage or defects within 10 days of delivery to qualify for free return shipping; reports made after 10 days may still be honored but the customer may be asked to cover return postage. Northbridge covers return shipping costs in full for any order over 75 dollars that qualifies for a standard return under Section 8.

## 12. Contact and Escalation

Employees with questions about leave balances should contact Human Resources through the internal HR portal or by emailing hr-leave@northbridge-example.com. Customers with questions about a return or refund should start with the online returns center; unresolved cases may be escalated to customer care, which responds to escalations within 2 business days. This policy is reviewed annually each January by the Human Resources and Customer Experience departments jointly.

## 13. Remote and Hybrid Employee Leave Provisions

Remote and hybrid employees accrue PTO and sick leave under the same rates described in Sections 3 and 4; location does not change the accrual formula. For remote staff, the leave day boundary follows the time zone registered as the employee's home office in the HR system, not the time zone of their manager or team. Remote employees must log any absence in the leave portal by 9 AM local time on the day of the absence, or as soon as reasonably possible for a same-day illness. Hybrid employees who split time between a Northbridge site and a home office follow the same 9 AM logging rule on remote days and may request leave for office days through their manager directly.

## 14. Frequently Asked Questions

Can PTO be taken in less than a full day? Yes, PTO may be used in half-day increments of 4 hours, subject to the same approval rules in Section 7. Can an employee donate unused sick leave to a coworker? Yes, through the Sick Leave Donation Program an employee may donate up to 3 sick days per benefit year to a colleague facing a documented medical hardship. What happens if a returned item is missing its original packaging? The receiving store may apply a 10 percent handling fee instead of the full 15 percent restocking fee described in Section 10, at the store manager's discretion. Does store credit ever expire early for inactive accounts? No; the 12-month expiration in Section 9 is fixed and does not change based on account activity.
