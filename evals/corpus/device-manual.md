# Boreal SmartBrew BX-9 Coffee Maker - User Manual

## 1. Overview and What Is in the Box

Thank you for purchasing the Boreal SmartBrew BX-9, a programmable countertop coffee maker with a built-in conical burr grinder and a 1.8-liter thermal carafe. The box contains one BX-9 base unit, one thermal carafe with lid, one removable water reservoir, one charcoal water filter cartridge, one cleaning brush for the grinder chute, and this manual. Before first use, inspect the packaging for shipping damage and confirm that the rating label on the underside of the base reads 120V, 60Hz, 1350W; do not operate the unit on any other voltage or frequency.

## 2. Safety Warnings

Never immerse the base unit, power cord, or plug in water or any other liquid. Always unplug the BX-9 before cleaning, before replacing the grinder burrs, and before leaving it unattended for an extended period. The brew basket and carafe become hot during operation; use the handle provided and avoid contact with the exposed metal warming plate, which can exceed 180 degrees Fahrenheit during a brew cycle. This appliance is not intended for use by children under 8 years of age, and children between 8 and 13 should be supervised. Keep the power cord away from hot surfaces and do not let it hang over the edge of a counter where it can be pulled down accidentally.

## 3. Setup and Installation

Place the BX-9 on a flat, dry, heat-resistant surface at least 6 inches from any wall or cabinet to allow proper steam venting. Remove the water reservoir by lifting it straight up from the base, rinse it with warm water, and reinstall the charcoal filter cartridge included in the box; the filter should sit flush in its cradle at the bottom of the reservoir. Fill the reservoir to a level between the MIN and MAX lines printed on the inside wall; the MAX line corresponds to twelve standard 5-ounce cups. Plug the base into a grounded outlet only after the reservoir and carafe are correctly seated, since the BX-9 will not power on if either part is missing.

## 4. Control Panel and Buttons

The control panel has five buttons and a backlit display. Pressing BREW starts an immediate brew using the last-selected strength and cup count. The STRENGTH button cycles through Mild, Classic, and Bold, changing the grind time and water-to-coffee ratio accordingly. The CUPS button sets the target yield from 2 to 12 cups in single-cup increments. The PROGRAM button opens the daily auto-start timer, letting you set an hour and minute for the machine to begin brewing automatically. The CLEAN button starts the descaling cycle described in Section 6. The display shows the current time, the selected cup count, and any active error code from the table in Section 7.

## 5. Operating Instructions

Begin a brew by filling the reservoir, adding whole beans to the bean hopper (up to 4 ounces), selecting strength and cup count, and pressing BREW. The grinder runs first, dispensing fresh grounds directly into the brew basket, followed by a pre-infusion bloom of approximately 30 seconds before full water flow begins. Brew water temperature is regulated between 195 and 205 degrees Fahrenheit throughout the cycle for optimal extraction. A full 12-cup brew takes approximately 9 minutes from the press of the BREW button to the final drip. The warming plate keeps the carafe at serving temperature for 35 minutes after brewing completes, after which the BX-9 automatically shuts off to save energy and reduce fire risk.

## 6. Cleaning and Descaling

Wipe the exterior with a damp cloth after each use and wash the brew basket and carafe lid by hand or on the top rack of a dishwasher. Brush out the grinder chute weekly using the included cleaning brush to prevent oil buildup from affecting grind consistency. Run the descaling cycle every 60 brew cycles, or every 3 months, whichever comes first; the display will also show error code E2 as a reminder once the internal cycle counter reaches that threshold. To descale, fill the reservoir with a mixture of equal parts water and white vinegar up to the MAX line, press and hold CLEAN for 3 seconds to start, and let the machine complete a full brew cycle with no coffee or filter installed, followed by two rinse cycles using plain water.

## 7. Troubleshooting

Error code E1 means the reservoir is empty or not seated correctly; refill it and press it firmly into place until it clicks. Error code E2 means the descaling threshold has been reached; follow the descaling procedure in Section 6. Error code E3 means the internal temperature sensor has detected an overheat condition; unplug the unit, let it cool for 30 minutes, and confirm the warming plate area is not obstructed before resuming use. Error code E4 means the lid on the water reservoir or the brew basket door is open; close both firmly until the display clears. Error code E5 means the internal pump has failed to prime after three attempts; contact support using the details in Section 10, since this fault is not user-serviceable.

## 8. Maintenance Schedule

Swap in a new charcoal water filter cartridge every 2 months or after 60 brewing cycles, whichever comes first, to keep chlorine taste and scale-forming minerals out of the brewed coffee. Inspect the grinder burrs every 6 months for wear; burrs that no longer produce a consistent grind should be replaced with manufacturer part number BX9-BURR-02. Check the door and lid gaskets annually for cracking or hardening, since a worn gasket can cause steam to escape and slow the brew cycle noticeably.

## 9. Technical Specifications

Electrically, the BX-9 operates at 120V, 60Hz, and draws 1350 watts at peak, primarily during the heating phase of a brew cycle. The thermal carafe holds 1.8 liters, equivalent to twelve 5-ounce cups. The bean hopper holds up to 4 ounces of whole beans, enough for roughly two full 12-cup brews before refilling. Overall dimensions are 14 inches tall, 8 inches wide, and 11 inches deep, and the unit weighs 9.2 pounds without water or beans loaded. The power cord measures 30 inches from the base to the plug.

## 10. Warranty and Support

Purchasing a Boreal SmartBrew BX-9 includes a 2-year limited warranty from the original date of purchase, covering defects in materials and workmanship under normal household use. The warranty does not cover damage from descaling neglect, use of non-potable water, or operation outside the rated 120V, 60Hz supply. To request service under warranty, keep your original receipt and contact Boreal customer support with your model number and a description of the fault; a support representative will confirm eligibility and arrange repair or replacement within 10 business days of an approved claim.

## 11. Accessories and Replacement Parts

Genuine replacement parts are sold directly through Boreal customer support and carry their own 90-day part warranty separate from the appliance warranty in Section 10. The replacement thermal carafe, part number BX9-CARAFE-01, fits only the BX-9 and is not interchangeable with earlier Boreal models. Replacement charcoal filter cartridges are sold in a 3-pack under part number BX9-FILT-3PK, a roughly six-month supply at the recommended 2-month replacement interval from Section 8. Owners who prefer to brew without paper or cartridge filters can order the optional gold-tone permanent filter, part number BX9-GOLD-01, which fits the standard brew basket and is dishwasher safe on the top rack.

## 12. Storage and Long-Term Care

If the BX-9 will sit unused for more than 2 weeks, empty the water reservoir completely, run one rinse cycle with plain water, and dry all removable parts before storing. Store the unit in a location between 50 and 90 degrees Fahrenheit; temperatures outside that range can degrade the internal seals and void the warranty described in Section 10. Never store the machine with a vinegar descaling solution left inside the reservoir, since residual acidity left for weeks can pit the internal pump housing well beyond what a normal descaling cycle would cause. Before long-term storage, also remove any beans left in the hopper, since stale oils can turn rancid and affect the flavor of the next brew.
